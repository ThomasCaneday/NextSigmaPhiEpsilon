Spring has sprung!

March was a busy month in the Tri Canyons. We wrapped up planting efforts in our current restoration site and would like to extend a huge THANK YOU to all of our volunteers that made it possible. We planted over 800 plants in this site over 5 months!


Don’t fret if you missed out on the planting bonanza. You can join us this month and ensure the longevity of the site by attending our next restoration event.


Our Canyon Coffee Event was a success and thank you again to the Friends of Tecolote Canyon for providing the bean juice.


We would also like to thank the SigEp Fraternity at USD for joining us at the Canyon Cleanup this month in Eastern Tecolote Canyon. It was truly a pleasant surprise, and we hope to see you at future events!  


Below are upcoming events you can join in April:

Habitat Restoration Volunteer Event

Saturday, April 6th

Tecolote Canyon 9am-11am


Location: Tecolote Restoration Site with the blue plant protector cones. Hike in from the Nature Center 1/2 mile down the trail. Coordinates: (32.774754, -117.189211)

Details: Gloves, tools, water, and snacks provided. We will be hand weeding and removing the protective cones from the native plants.

Creek to Bay Cleanup Day (Organized by I Love a Clean San Diego)

Saturday, April 20th

North Tecolote Canyon 9am-12pm


Location: North Clairemont Recreation Center (4421 Bannock Ave, San Diego)

Details: Across San Diego County there are 100 locations of cleanup sites that you can register for by clicking on the button below. If you would like to see a familiar face or help out in your local canyon you can come to the North Clairemont Rec Center site! Wear sturdy shoes and bring a water bottle, everything else will be provided.

Information About Creek to Bay Cleanup Day

Earth Day is Monday, April 22nd and while there are no organized events taking place in the Tri Canyons there are plenty of other ways to celebrate!


We recommend trying to find time to get outside to appreciate the natural spaces in our community. More options include educating yourself about local environmental issues, pledging to lower your climate impact, or having a meaningful conversation with friends about how much you love Tecolote Canyon the Earth.  

As always, feel free to email tricanyonsvolunteering@sandiego.gov with any questions! Happy trails.
