Tecolote Canyon Cleanup



CalBetaBeta Efforts

Over the past semester, members of the Cal Beta Beta chapter dedicated their time and effort to support the restoration of Tecolote Canyon. Collaborating closely with the City of San Diego's National Parks and Wildlife organization, our members diligently worked to remove debris and litter around the canyon and along walking trails. Among the various items we encountered, including rusty bikes, bows, and blades, our community-driven cleanup efforts aimed to preserve the natural beauty of this unique national park for all to enjoy.



Importance of Tecalote Canyon


Tecolote Canyon Natural Park serves as a vital remnant of San Diego's diverse ecological and cultural heritage. This cherished landscape, once stewarded by the Kumeyaay tribes, continues to be a living connection to the region's indigenous roots. The canyon's native plant species, some of which are now endangered, form a delicate habitat that supports local wildlife. The trails and greenery are nestled within an urban setting, providing a green escape that intertwines nature with the community fabric of the surrounding neighborhoods. Through collective efforts to maintain and restore Tecolote Canyon, we honor its past, protect its present, and secure its future, underscoring the canyon's role as a natural sanctuary amidst city life and as a testament to the enduring legacy of its original caretakers.
